Welcome to SecureAI

The Usage of AI in prevention of Social Engineering Attacks project is an advanced, comprehensive cybersecurity platform designed to identify, analyze, and thwart various forms of human-targeted cyber threats. By leveraging cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning techniques, this system proactively defends against the psychological manipulation tactics commonly employed by modern cybercriminals.

In today's digital landscape, social engineering attacks such as phishing, smishing, and quishing exploit human vulnerability rather than technical flaws. Our project aims to bridge this security gap by providing a multi-layered defense mechanism. It evaluates communications and user behaviors in real-time, serving as a vigilant digital guardian that empowers users to navigate the web safely and securely.

1. SMS Spam Detector

Logic: NLP tokenization and contextual sequence analysis.

Model: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash AI model.

Training Data: Pre-trained on vast datasets of global SMS communications, both legitimate and spam.

Functionality: Real-time classification of SMS messages to determine if they are malicious phishing attempts or safe ('ham').

Example (Spam): "Congratulations! You won a free gift. Click to claim now"
Example (Ham): "Hi! Are we still meeting for dinner tonight?"

2. QR Code Threat Scanner

Logic: Image processing to decode QR matrices followed by rigorous URL threat extraction and analysis.

Algorithm & Models: OpenCV for robust QR decoding; LangChain combined with Google Gemini 2.5 Flash for URL threat classification.

Training Data: Analyzes patterns derived from known phishing URLs, safe domain registries, and malicious link structures.

Functionality: Scans QR codes and classifies the hidden URL as Safe, Phishing, or Malicious.

Example (Safe): A restaurant's digital menu URL.
Example (Phishing): A QR code routing to a deceptive banking login page.

3. Email Phishing Analyzer

Logic: Textual analysis focusing on psychological triggers like urgency, authority, and deceptive phrasing.

Model: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash.

Training Data: Generalized knowledge base encompassing historical phishing templates and standard corporate/personal communications.

Functionality: Evaluates email contents and returns a strict classification (Safe, Suspicious, Phishing) along with bulleted explanations.

Example (Phishing): "Dear customer, your account will be locked in 24 hours. Click here to verify."
Example (Suspicious): "Hi team, please find attached the meeting notes from yesterday's sync."

4. Password Strength & Breach Analyzer

Logic: Combines heuristic complexity checking with secure, zero-knowledge breach verification.

Algorithm: SHA-1 hashing algorithm for k-Anonymity (prefix matching).

Data Source: Integrates with the "Have I Been Pwned" API database.

Functionality: Evaluates password strength and checks for exposure in known data breaches without ever transmitting plain text.

Example (Weak/Breached): password123
Example (Strong/Safe): Tr0ub4dor&3!Secure

5. User Behavior Anomaly Detection

Logic: Statistical profiling and machine learning classification to detect atypical session parameters.

Algorithms & Models: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost classifiers.

Training Data: Trained locally on behavior_anomaly_dataset.csv, learning from historical login hours and IP risk scores.

Classification Details: The models evaluate login_hour (time of day) and ip_score (probabilistic risk score). High risk IPs combined with atypical login hours (e.g., 3:00 AM) are flagged as an Anomaly. Standard business hours with low IP scores are classified as Normal.

Normal Behavior:
login_hour = 10
ip_score = 0.20
Anomaly Detected:
login_hour = 2
ip_score = 0.95
